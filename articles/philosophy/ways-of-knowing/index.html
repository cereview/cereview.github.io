Philosophy in its etymological roots comes from two greek words meaning friendly or loving (\(\phi\iota\lambda os\), pronounced philos) and knowledge (\(\rho o \phi\iota\alpha\), sophia). Originally it connoted love of wisdom, a desire for fresh experience, or the pursuit of intellectual culture. It later came to denote the disciplined study of reality and of human nature. The earliest greek philosophers Socrates (c. 470-399 B.C.), Plato (c. 428/427-348/347 B.C.), and Aristotle (c. 384-322 B.C.) contributed greatly to the Western Philosophy.
